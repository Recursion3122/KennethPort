Roleplay

As part of our BSED-English class, we recently participated in a role-playing activity that centered around the concept of needing a chaperone. The scenario involved us, the students, approaching a parent or teacher and requesting their presence as a chaperone on a field trip.

This role-playing exercise helped me understand the significance of chaperones in a very useful way. From the viewpoint of the student, a chaperone is essential to guaranteeing our security and welfare whether we are going on excursions or attending activities. They offer a feeling of protection and oversight, particularly in strange places. Chaperones can also serve as additional resources, responding to inquiries and providing further assistance during the trip.

The role-playing exercise itself turned out to be a great means of refining our interpersonal and communication abilities. We were able to practice approaching an adult, stating our case with clarity, and elucidating the need of the chaperone's presence. The experience was interesting and educational, illuminating the importance of chaperones for student excursions.

Conclusion: A Journey in Words


This portfolio is more than just a collection of assignments; it's a testament to the incredible journey I've taken through Philippine and English Literature. Stepping into EL108 felt like entering a vast library filled with stories waiting to be explored. Filipino myths and legends resonated with a familiarity that surprised me. English literature, on the other handâ€” opened doors to worlds both familiar and fantastical. Shakespeare's sonnets unveiled the power of language to weave emotions.

EL109 became a workshop where I could experiment with the tools I'd gathered. Analyzing literary techniques wasn't just about memorizing terms; it was about understanding the machinery behind storytelling. The freedom to write creatively, whether it was crafting a personal essay or weaving a fictional tale, was exhilarating. I discovered the joy of getting lost in the flow of my own imagination, and the satisfaction of revising and polishing my work until it gleamed.

There were moments of frustration, of course. Staring at a blank page, wrestling with writer's block, feeling like my ideas weren't good enough. But with each challenge, I learned to persevere. I discovered the importance of research, of seeking inspiration from diverse sources, and of embracing feedback as a chance to grow.

Looking back, EL108 and EL109 have been more than just classes; they've been a catalyst for my creativity. I've learned that literature isn't just about reading; it's about absorbing, interpreting, and using the power of words to create. This portfolio is a snapshot of that journey, a testament to the lessons learned and the stories waiting to be told.
